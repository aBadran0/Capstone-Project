About Our Project

Understanding and Visualizing Atmospheric Gases in the UAE

Introduction

The intersection of computer science and climatology is at the forefront for a new era of environmental insight, where technology and earth sciences converge to create powerful tools for understanding the nature of the world around us. Our project leverages the technique of remote sensing to retrieve a wealth of data on atmospheric gases, focusing specifically on the Middle East, with a spotlight on the United Arab Emirates.

The Power of Sentinel-5 Precursor and Remote Sensing

Leveraging the TROPOMI sensor aboard the Sentinel-5 Precursor satellite, we gather essential insights into air quality and atmospheric conditions, crucial for monitoring environmental changes efficiently and accurately.

Interactive Geospatial Mapping

This key feature of our project involves the development of an interactive map layer that overlays comprehensive metrics onto a conventional map, providing visual representations of environmental health across different areas.

Integration with Ground Station Data

To enhance our observations, we integrate ground station data through the OpenAQ API, complementing our satellite data to ensure accuracy in our Air Quality Index (AQI) calculations.

Motivation and Goals

Motivated by a need to bridge the gap between complex data and public accessibility, our project transforms scientific data into intelligible insights that empower both laymen and policymakers to make informed decisions, fostering a deeper understanding of environmental risks and advocating for better air quality.

Impact and Vision

Our project's benefits extend to enhancing public health and safety by making complex data comprehensible, aiding policy making and urban planning, and raising environmental awareness to encourage proactive actions towards sustainability.
